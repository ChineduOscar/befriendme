Human needs theory was proposed by Abraham Maslow, in 1943 paper ‘A Theory of Human Motivation” in psychological review. Maslow and John Burton, hold that human need a number of essentials to survive, and that these essentials go beyond food, water and shelter. They include both physical and non-physical elements needed for human growth and development, as well as thing humans are innately driven to attain. These needs are hierarchical in nature they are; physiological, safety, belongings, self-esteem, self-actualization. Burton and others contends that needs do not have hierarchical order rather needs are sought simultaneously in an intense and relentless manner. We have safety, belongingness/love, personal fulfillment, cultural security, freedom, distributive justice, and participation.
